Detailed Sorting Information

Learn how to properly sort different types of waste for effective recycling.

Plastic

Plastics are versatile materials made from polymers. They should be clean and dry before recycling.

What goes in

  • Plastic bottles (water, soda, etc.)
  • Food containers and lids
  • Plastic bags and wraps
  • Shampoo and detergent bottles
  • Yogurt and margarine tubs
  • Plastic cutlery (if accepted locally)

Tips

  • Check for recycling numbers (1-7) on the bottom
  • Remove caps and rinse containers
  • Compress bottles to save space
  • Not all plastics are recyclable - check local guidelines

Glass

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly without loss in quality or purity.

What goes in

  • Glass bottles (all colors)
  • Glass jars and containers
  • Sauce and condiment bottles
  • Wine and beer bottles
  • Baby food jars
  • Cosmetic glass containers

Tips

  • Rinse containers thoroughly
  • Remove lids and caps (recycle separately)
  • No need to remove labels
  • Don't include window glass, mirrors, or ceramics

Metal

Metals are highly valuable recyclable materials that can be melted down and reformed repeatedly.

What goes in

  • Aluminum cans
  • Steel and tin cans
  • Metal lids and bottle caps
  • Clean aluminum foil
  • Empty aerosol cans
  • Metal cookware (if accepted locally)

Tips

  • Rinse food residue from cans
  • Flatten aluminum cans to save space
  • Remove paper labels when possible
  • Empty aerosol cans completely

Cardboard

Cardboard is made from paper pulp and is highly recyclable when clean and dry.

What goes in

  • Corrugated cardboard boxes
  • Shipping boxes
  • Cereal and food boxes
  • Shoe boxes
  • Pizza boxes (clean portions only)
  • Toilet paper and paper towel tubes

Tips

  • Flatten boxes to save space
  • Remove tape and stickers when possible
  • Keep dry to prevent mold
  • Greasy pizza boxes should be composted

Paper

Paper can be recycled 5-7 times before fibers become too short to be used again.

What goes in

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Office paper and envelopes
  • Junk mail and catalogs
  • Phone books
  • Paper bags
  • Wrapping paper (non-metallic)

Tips

  • Remove plastic wrapping from magazines
  • Keep paper dry and clean
  • Shredded paper may need special handling
  • No paper towels, tissues, or napkins

Trash

Items that cannot be recycled and must go to landfill or incineration.

What goes in

  • Food waste (if no compost available)
  • Plastic wrap and film
  • Styrofoam packaging
  • Diapers and sanitary products
  • Broken ceramics and dishware
  • Light bulbs (non-LED)

Tips

  • Reduce waste by choosing recyclable alternatives
  • Consider composting food waste
  • Check for special disposal programs for electronics
  • Hazardous waste needs special disposal

Did you know?

Plastic bags and other plastic garbage thrown into the ocean kill as many as 1 million sea creatures every year.
